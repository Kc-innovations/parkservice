Who we are

The Nigeria National Park Service (NPS) is a federal agency under the Federal Ministry of Environment, dedicated to the conservation and sustainable management of Nigeria’s national parks and unique biodiversity. Established by law in 1991 through Decree 36 (now Act Cap L65, Laws of the Federation 2004), the NPS was created to unify and standardize the management of key conservation areas across the country.

Mission

“To manage and regulate the use of the nation’s national parks, conserve biodiversity, provide environmental education, and promote ecotourism through sound policies, research, and collaboration with stakeholders.”

Vision

"To be a world-class conservation authority, preserving Nigeria’s rich natural heritage and biodiversity through sustainable management for the benefit of present and future generations."

Our Statutory Mandate

  • Conserve biodiversity including endangered species.
  • Promote ecotourism and environmental education.
  • Enforce wildlife laws and collaborate with security agencies.
  • Conduct research and monitor ecosystems.
  • Engage local communities through park-friendly policies.

Conservator General's Message

It is with great pride and a deep sense of responsibility that I welcome you to the Nigeria National Park Service. As the agency mandated to safeguard Nigeria’s most treasured natural ecosystems, our mission goes beyond protecting flora and fauna—we are also preserving the nation’s environmental legacy and promoting sustainable development for generations to come.


Nigeria is blessed with extraordinary biodiversity, ranging from tropical rainforests and savannahs to wetlands and mountainous regions. Our seven national parks represent the heart of this natural heritage. They are sanctuaries for endangered species, reservoirs of genetic diversity, and living laboratories for ecological research and education. As such, the National Park Service remains at the forefront of conservation efforts, policy implementation, and global environmental collaboration.


In today’s rapidly changing world, the importance of protected areas cannot be overstated. Climate change, deforestation, poaching, and habitat encroachment continue to challenge our resolve. Yet, through innovation, strengthened partnerships, community engagement, and dedicated enforcement, we remain steadfast in our commitment to preserve Nigeria’s natural treasures.


We are also positioning our parks as key drivers of ecotourism, environmental education, and sustainable livelihoods. By encouraging responsible tourism and public participation, we aim to build a conservation culture that resonates in every household and community across Nigeria.


I invite all stakeholders—government bodies, local communities, civil society, researchers, and visitors—to join us in this noble task. Together, we can ensure that Nigeria’s national parks not only survive but thrive as symbols of pride, peace, and progress.


Dr. Ibrahim Goni

Conservator-General
